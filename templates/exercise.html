Exercise Guide for Diabetes

Regular physical activity is crucial for managing diabetes. Here's your comprehensive guide to staying active and healthy.

Aerobic Exercise (Cardio)

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.

Walking

A simple and accessible option for everyone. Start with 10-minute walks and gradually increase duration.

Swimming

Excellent low-impact exercise that's gentle on your joints while providing full-body workout.

Cycling

Great for cardiovascular health and can be done indoors or outdoors.

Dancing

A fun and engaging way to get your heart rate up and improve fitness.

Strength Training

Include strength training at least two days per week.

Weight Lifting

Start with light weights and focus on proper form

Resistance Bands

Versatile equipment for home workouts

Bodyweight Exercises

Squats, push-ups, and other exercises using your body weight

Flexibility and Balance

Yoga

Improves flexibility and reduces stress

Tai Chi

Enhances balance and coordination

Stretching

Maintains flexibility and prevents injury

Important Considerations

  • Monitor blood sugar before, during, and after exercise
  • Wear comfortable, supportive shoes and check feet regularly
  • Stay well-hydrated throughout your workout
  • Consult healthcare professional before starting new exercise routine
  • Start slowly and gradually increase intensity
